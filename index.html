An investigation into the friction between human intuition and computational evaluation (AI) in early-stage financing.



01 — Problem Statement

The Gap

Human Bias

Venture screening is currently an art form, relying heavily on intuition. It is prone to cognitive biases, scales poorly, and lacks consistency in defining "potential."

Algorithmic Promise

Large Language Models offer scale. The research questions if algorithms can be recalibrated to mimic expert logic to provide an objective "second opinion."

03 — Methodology

Roadmap

STUDY I In Progress

Policy Delphi Study

Establishing a consensus definition of "idea potential." We engage experts to deconstruct criteria used in high-stakes financing decisions.



STUDY II

LLM Recalibration

Training language models to follow instructions derived from expert consensus. Benchmarking LLM performance against human VCs.

STUDY III

Domain-Logic Alignment

Investigating context. Comparing VC funding, public funding, and arts funding to understand how "potential" shifts across domains.

STUDY IV

Hybrid Framework

Developing a "Human-in-the-loop" system where AI acts as a mediator. This includes a blueprint for the development of a domain-specific SLM for idea evaluation.

04 — Collaborators

I collaborate with institutional bodies and experts possessing a deep understanding of financing potential.
